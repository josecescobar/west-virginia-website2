Explore West Virginia Facts

Natural Wonders

  • Only state completely within Appalachian Mountain range
  • Home to over 1,000 caves and caverns
  • Contains the world's second-largest steel arch bridge
  • Features over 1,500 miles of hiking trails

Historical Firsts

  • First rural free mail delivery (Charles Town, 1896)
  • First state formed during the Civil War
  • First brick street in world (Charleston, 1870s)
  • First Mother's Day celebration (Grafton, 1908)

Unique Attractions

  • The Greenbrier's secret Cold War bunker
  • America's oldest dime store (Berdine's, est. 1908)
  • Palace of Gold - "America's Taj Mahal"
  • World's largest steerable telescope (Green Bank)

Cultural Heritage

  • Birthplace of Pearl S. Buck (Nobel laureate)
  • Home to the Golden Delicious Apple
  • Origin of pepperoni rolls
  • Inspiration for "Take Me Home, Country Roads"

Did You Know?

West Virginia was originally going to be named "Kanawha." Click to reveal why the name was changed!

The name was changed to West Virginia to maintain the historical connection to Virginia while asserting its separate identity as a Union state during the Civil War.
